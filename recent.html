Recent Success and Failures of Michigan Football

Since 1997 Michigan football has been the subject of many ups and downs.

1997 National Championship

In coach Lloyd Carr's 3rd season as head coached a Charles Woodson and Brian Greise led team to an undefeated record and win in the 1998 Rose Bowl. The wolverines finised as #1 in the AP poll and grabbed its first championship since 1948

Charles Woodson

The star of the 1997 national championship team was also the first and only defensive player to ever win the heisman trophy, given to the nations most outstanding player.

Tom Brady

Known as the greatest QB of all time by many, Tom Brady played his college career at Michigan where he led the team to a Big Ten championship in 1998.

"Game of the Century

After a few Rose bowl appearances in the early 2000's #2 Michigan faced off against #1 Ohio St in a game some dubbed as the "Game of the Century" in 2006. Ohio St pulled off the 42-39 win and went to the national championship, while Michigan appeared in the rose bowl and lost to USC.

"Llyod Carr's Final Game

In 2007 Carr announced he would be retiring after the team's bowl game following a loss to Ohio State. In the 2008 Capital One bowl the team beat Tim Tebow and the defending national champion Florida Gators 41-35

"Rich Rodriguez

Following Carr's retirement Michigan hired spread-offense guru, Rich Rodriguez from West Virginia. Things did not go as planned as Micigan went 3-9 in his first season and missed a bowl game for the schools first time since 1974. Things did not improve and "Rich Rod" was fired 2 years later in 2010.

"Brady Hoke

In 2011 the school announced the hiring of Michigan alum, Brady Hoke. In his first season he led the team to 11 wins including an appearence in the Sugar Bowl and a win against Ohio St. Things went downhill from there and Hoke was fired in 2014 after a losing season.

"Jim Harbaugh

In 2015 the school hired former QB under Bo and former Stanford and 49ers head coach, Jim Harbaugh. With the hire came high expectations and he has mostly lived up to them so far with a total record of 47-18.
