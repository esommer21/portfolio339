History of Michigan Football

A brief history of the winningiest and one of the most prominent college football programs in history.

Beginnings

Michigan played its first intercollegiate game on May 30th, 1879 against Racine College in Chicago. The School won its first conference championship in 1898 against Chicago, inspiring the song "The Victors" which ultimately became the schools fight song.

Fielding Yost Years (1901-1926)

In his first year as head coach the team participated in the first ever Rose Bowl and dominanted Stanford. From 1901-1904 Michigan did not lose a single game. Prior to stepping asside to become athletic director in 1926 Yost's teams won national championships in 1901, 1902, 1903, 1904, 1918 and 1923 while posting a 165-29-10 record. Additionally, while atheletic director he oversaw the construction of Michigan Stadium (initially holding 72,00)
